Mercury

Mercury

2000s Missions

Messenger

Perhaps a stretch of an acronym, (MErcury Surface, Space ENvironment, GEochemistry, and Ranging), MESSENGER was the second NASA spacecraft sent to Mercury (the first being Mariner 10). It launched in 2004, took 7 years to insert into orbit around Mercury, and ended in 2015 via crashing onto the surface. A couple of notable accomplishments include that it has been the first spacecraft to orbit Mercury, and has mapped the surface of the planet in great detail.

Venus

Venus

2000s Missions

Venus Express

The Venus Express was ESA's first spacecraft sent to Venus. Its goal was to investiate the planet's clouds and atmosphere. It also made temperature maps of Venus. Launched in 2005, it concluded its mission in 2014, having run out of fuel.

Moon

Moon

2000s Missions

Chandrayaan

Chandrayaan-1 was the ISRO (India)'s very first deep-space mission, as well as first lunar lander, and it was a success. It paved the way for future Chandrayaan lunar missions in later years.

2000s Missions

Chang'e

Chang'e-1 was the CNSA's (China's) first deep-space mission & first lunar lander, just like Chandrayaan for the ISRO. Many more successes with the Chang'e program would occur in the next decade.

2000s Missions

Kaguya (Selene)

Kaguya was a lunar orbiter developed by JAXA that studied lunar history as well as paved the way technology-wise for future lunar spacecraft.

2000s Missions

Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter

This NASA orbiter launched with another called LCROSS and travelled to the moon. Launched in 2009, it is still active today. It's been responsible for mapping the Moon and taking measurements of composition, radiation, and temperature, with greater accuracy and detail than previous missions.

2000s Missions

LCROSS

Lunar Crater Observation and Sensing Satellite, or LCROSS, was a NASA orbiter sent to the moon. It hitched a ride with the LRO, with which it shared a primary goal of finding water ice on the Moon. It launched in 2009 and within the same year, later intentionally impacted the lunar surface.

le spacecraft

2000s Missions

Smart-1

Smart-1 was ESA's first mission to the Moon. It was intended to learn more about the Moon and its history, and to test a new technology for future missions: solar-electric propulsion.

Mars

Mars

2000s Missions

2001 Mars Odyssey

This Martian orbiter, created by NASA, is one of 5 in the Mars Relay Network. On top of its own science and observation duties, it provides communications assitance between NASA and ESA Martian spacecraft to Earth.

And it's still active and operating!

2000s Missions

Mars Express

This is another Martian orbiter that is part of the Mars Relay Network. But this one is different because it was made by ESA, and it was their very first mission to Mars. It's been a big success for them, and continues to study the planet and report back findings.

2000s Missions

Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter

This NASA orbiter was launched in 2005, arriving to Mars in 2006. Like lots of Mars missions of the time, it was focused on the presence of water, and potential to have supported life. It's still active today, and is one of the 5 spacecraft in the Mars Relay Network.

If there's one thing this mission has taught me, it's how to properly spell 'reconnaissance'.

2000s Missions

Phoenix

Phoenix was a NASA Mars lander that was sent to search for evidence of past living microbes there. This lander's samples that it collected contributed to NASA announcing the confirmed presence of water on Mars.

2000s Missions

Spirit & Opportunity

These two twin rovers, developed by NASA, went to Mars in 2003. Both landed successfully and explored various rocks and soil types, and studied the history of water on the planet, which could indicate if it was previously capable of supporting life.

Asteroids & Comets

Asteroids & Comets

2000s Missions

Dawn

Dawn was a NASA orbiter spacecraft that travelled to the asteroid belt (between Mars and Jupiter). It was the first to explore both of its targets, a giant asteroid called Vesta as well as the dwarf planet Ceres. It operated from launch in 2007 to its conclusion in 2018.

le spacecraft

2000s Missions

Hayabusa & Minerva

Hayabusa-1 & MINERVA were Japanese spacecraft that travelled to the asteroid Itokawa. Hayabusa-1 accomplished the first asteroid sample return to Earth, and was also the first spacecraft to successfully land & take off from an asteroid. Both it and MINERVA also served as tech demos. MINERVA itself was a rover that was meant to hop around, take pictures, and measure temperature, but it did not land on the asteroid.

le spacecraft

2000s Missions

Rosetta & Philae

Rosetta & Philae were an ESA orbiter-lander duo that travelled together to a comet, with Philae landing on it. ESA also dubs Rosetta as a 'comet chaser', which sounds awesome. The mission has since ended, but it accomplished a lot of comet-exploration firsts, for ESA and for the world.

2000s Missions

New Horizons

This was the first - and only, as far as I can tell - spacecraft to be sent to explore Pluto up close. It's been the first spacecraft of the New Frontiers program, which later included Juno and OSIRIS-REx.

And it's still active today!

Telescopes

Telescopes

2000s Missions

Herschel

Developed by ESA, this space telescope launched along with another ESA telescope, Planck, in 2009. It was advanced for its time and studied the formation & evolution of stars and galaxies.

le spacecraft

2000s Missions

Kepler

Kepler was a NASA telescope that focused on discovering exoplanets and exosystems within the Milky Way. It launched in 2009, and operated until 2018. Throughout its lifetime, 2,600 exoplanets were discovered by it. Though it is finished now, the search and study of exoplanets continues with another space telescope, TESS, which launched in 2018.

2000s Missions

Planck

Another ESA space telescope, Planck focused on exploring space history via studying the Cosmic Microwave Background. This was ESA's first CMB-studying spacecraft.
