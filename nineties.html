Note: from here on out I'm not going to be covering manned missions, simply because I'd rather focus on the robotic missions. Because there's a lot. But in the background, and sometimes in support of robotic missions, there are manned missions, primarily focused on space station presence & science.

ROBOTIC MISSIONS

ACE

STATUS: ACTIVE

DEVELOPED BY: NASA

ACE, short for Advanced Composition Explorer, launched in 1997 and is still active today. It studies interactions between the Earth and the Sun.

CASSINI-HUYGENS

STATUS: COMPLETE

DEVELOPED BY: NASA, ESA

Cassini was a NASA orbiter, and the Titan lander Huygens was from ESA. Both travelled together to the Saturn system in 1997, but wouldn't arrive until years later. While Huygens burned up at the end of its descent (which was expected), Cassini continued its mission of observing and studying the Saturn system up until 2015.

CLEMENTINE

STATUS: COMPLETE

DEVELOPED BY: NASA, Ballistic Missile Defense Organization

This was the first lunar mission by America in two decades, since Explorer 49. Its main focus was testing newly-developed low-cost engineering technology, such as sensors and solar panels.

DEEP SPACE

STATUS: COMPLETE

DEVELOPED BY: NASA

Deep Space 1 served as a tech demo as well as a flyby to an asteroid called 9660 Braille. One of the cool technologies it was testing was autonomous flight software. It launched in 1998, completed its primary mission, then was given an extended mission to the comet Borrelly.

GEOTAIL

STATUS: COMPLETE

DEVELOPED BY: NASA, Japan

Geotail launched in 1992 to study the Earth's magnetosphere. It operated for decades, only just recently finishing up in 2022.

HUBBLE

STATUS: ACTIVE

DEVELOPED BY: NASA

You have probably heard of this one before. The Hubble Space Telescope, launched in 1990, has taken more than 1.7 million observations, and is the reason for many modern famous pictures of the universe. It celebrated 35 years of operation this year.

LUNAR PROSPECTOR

STATUS: COMPLETE

DEVELOPED BY: NASA

The Lunar Prospector's objectives were to map the composition of the Moon's surface and to look for water ice, suspected to be at the poles. It lasted from 1998 to 1999, upon which it was intentionally crashed into the Moon.

MARS GLOBAL SURVEYOR

STATUS: COMPLETE

DEVELOPED BY: NASA

Mars Global Surveyor was an orbiter focused on studying the Martian surface, atmosphere, and interior. It launched in 1996, arrived the next year, and met an abrupt end in 2006 when it had a major computer error.

MARS PATHFINDER + SOJOURNER

STATUS: COMPLETE

DEVELOPED BY: NASA

Mars Pathfinder and Sojourner were launched together in 1996 and arrived to Mars in 1997. Pathfinder was a lander, and its purpose was to be a tech demo as well as safely deliver the Sojourner rover. Pathfinder returned over 16,000 images. It was also uniquely the first Martian spacecraft to test the new airbag landing technique.

Sojourner was the very first Martian rover of any nation, and it surpassed its initial mission, going on to survive for 3 months on the Red Planet, and sent back more than 500 images.

Sojourner is now my personal favorite Martian rover. Simply because it is so small and cute (it measured about 2ft long/1.5ft wide/1 ft tall).

NEAR SHOEMAKER

STATUS: COMPLETE

DEVELOPED BY: NASA

This was the first US spacecraft to be the first to land on a celestial body. In this case, it was the asteroid called Eros. It also accomplished the first asteroid landing of any spacecraft.

SOHO

STATUS: ACTIVE

DEVELOPED BY: NASA, ESA

Short for Solar and Heliospheric Observatory, this mission launched mid-decade in 1995, and is still active today. It has informed scientist a lot about how the Sun works, and its observations are used to forecast solar storms. It's also discovered more than 5,000 comets during its time.

STARDUST

STATUS: COMPLETE

DEVELOPED BY: NASA

Stardust was focused on comets and asteroids. Its major accomplishment was that it was the first spacecraft to bring back samples from a comet.

ULYSSES

STATUS: COMPLETE

DEVELOPED BY: NASA, ESA

Ulysses was launched at the very beginning of the decade in 1990, and its focus was the Sun. In particular, it was focused on the ares near the Solar North & South poles. It was the first spacecraft to focus on & observe these areas.

WIND

STATUS: ACTIVE

DEVELOPED BY: NASA

Wind is an orbiter that was launched in 1994 to study solar wind. It's still active today, and apparently has enough fuel to last it for decades to come.
