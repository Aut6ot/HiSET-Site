FUTURE

2030 and Beyond

What lies ahead for space exploration?

Manned Space Programs

These programs seem to be starting up all across the board.

There's Artemis, which primarly involves NASA, CSA (Canada), ESA, JAXA, and the Mohammed Bin Rashid Space Center, as well as a whole host of commercial companies involved through contracts.

The CNSA has their own space station they've constructed a few years ago (Tiangong), and seem to be advancing their manned space program, known as CMS for China Manned Space.

ISRO is currently developing their manned space program, called Gaganyaan.

Generally, there seems to be a big push to land humans on Mars. This is tricky though; According to this estimate, it could cost about half a trillion dollars!

Robotic Missions

I've listed below a few robotic missions that are expected to launch 2030 and beyond. However, a lot can happen, and it's quite possible many of these get delayed or even just never happen.

VERITAS and DAVINCI (I'll spare you their full names) are two separate missions, but both are geared towards exploring Venus, which has kinda been neglected since the '90s.

A NASA mission to Uranus (Uranus Orbiter and Probe) was proposed a few years ago. But plenty of missions get proposed and not executed. But I would love to see it happen.

And generally, there will probably be continuations of projects like Gaganyaan, Chang'e, and Tianwen.
